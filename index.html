Padeye Help

What this tool does: verifies a padeye (lifting lug) by checking bearing, shear-out, net-section tension, cheek-weld shear, and reports the governing utilisation (UC). The schematic canvas shows geometry and load direction.

Inputs

  • dp – pin diameter.
  • dh – hole diameter (clearance).
  • rm – outer radius of the main plate arc.
  • rc – outer radius for cheek plates (if used).
  • tm – main plate thickness.
  • tc – cheek plate thickness (both sides assumed).
  • w – fillet weld leg size for cheeks.
  • ℓ – ligament length used for tear-out (c-c).
  • fy – steel yield strength (N/mm²).
  • Loading & v – design load and factor. The product is Fdl.
  • α – load angle for the arrow on the graphic (informational).

Results

  • Bearing at contact: \( f_p = F_{dl} / (d_p\,(t_m+2t_c)) \) ≤ 0.9·fy.
  • Shear at a-a: \( f_s = 1.1·0.5·F_{dl} / A_{a'} \) ≤ 0.4·fy, where \( A_{a'}=(r_m-0.5d_h)t_m+2(r_c-0.5d_h)t_c \).
  • Tension at b-b: \( f_a = F_{dl} / A_{b'} \) ≤ 0.45·fy, with \( A_{b'}=2A_{a'} \).
  • Cheek-weld shear: throat area \( A_w = 2πr_c·w/√2 \); load per cheek \( F' = t_c·F_{dl}/(t_m+2t_c) \); check \( 2F' / A_w ≤ 0.4·fy \).
  • Tear-out at c-c: \( f_s = F_{dl}/(t_m·ℓ) \) ≤ 0.4·fy.
  • UC = demand / allowable; PASS if UC ≤ 1.0.

How to use

  1. Enter geometry and material values (mm and N/mm²).
  2. Set the design load and factor to get Fdl.
  3. Review PASS/FAIL. The “Governing UC” card shows the worst case.
  4. Use the graphic to sanity-check proportions and load direction.

Typical detailing tips

  • Provide hole clearance (dh > dp) suitable for the pin or shackle.
  • Ensure cheeks fit the pin jaw width if applicable.
  • Adopt sensible radii and fillets to avoid sharp stress raisers.
  • Pick weld sizes that meet both strength and practical fabrication limits.

Troubleshooting

  • High bearing UC → increase thickness (tm/tc) or pin diameter, or reduce load.
  • Shear a-a failing → increase radii (rm/rc) or thicknesses.
  • Net tension failing → same as above; remember \(A_{b'}=2A_{a'}\).
  • Weld check failing → increase weld leg w or cheek radius rc.
  • Tear-out failing → increase ℓ or tm.

This tool supports preliminary and detailed checks with code-style allowables. Apply engineering judgement and project-specific requirements.
